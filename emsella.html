Frequently Asked Questions

Is Emsella painful or uncomfortable?

No. The sensation is unusual — you'll feel intense pelvic floor contractions and a tingling feeling — but it's not painful. Most patients adjust quickly and find it tolerable, even relaxing. The intensity is adjustable to your comfort level.

Do I have to undress?

No! This is one of the biggest advantages of Emsella. You remain fully clothed throughout treatment. Simply wear comfortable clothing (avoid jeans with metal buttons/zippers) and sit on the chair.

How soon will I see results?

Many patients notice improvement after 2-3 sessions. You might experience fewer leakage episodes, stronger urges before accidents, or improved control. Optimal results appear 2-4 weeks after completing the full protocol as muscles continue strengthening.

How long do results last?

Results can last 6-12 months or longer with proper maintenance. Many patients return for single maintenance sessions every 6-12 months to sustain results. Continuing pelvic floor exercises at home also helps maintain strength.

Can I do Emsella if I have a pacemaker or IUD?

Pacemakers are a contraindication for Emsella due to the electromagnetic technology. IUDs (copper or hormonal) are generally fine, but we'll discuss your specific situation during consultation. Other contraindications include pregnancy, metal implants in the treatment area, and certain medical conditions.

Is this covered by insurance?

Currently, Emsella is considered an elective treatment and is not typically covered by insurance. However, given the impact on quality of life, many patients find the investment worthwhile. We can discuss pricing and package options during your consultation.

How is this different from Kegel exercises?

Emsella delivers 11,000 supramaximal contractions in 28 minutes — far more than you could achieve voluntarily. These contractions are stronger and deeper, reaching muscles you can't consciously engage. Plus, Emsella ensures proper form (many people do Kegels incorrectly) and provides consistent, targeted strengthening.

What about the sexual health benefits?

Stronger pelvic floor muscles improve sexual function in both women and men. Women report increased sensation, stronger orgasms, and reduced vaginal laxity. Men experience improved erectile function, better control, and enhanced satisfaction. These benefits result from the same muscle strengthening that addresses incontinence.

Will Emsella help with waking up multiple times at night to urinate?

Yes! Many patients, especially men, report significant reduction in nighttime bathroom trips (nocturia). By strengthening the pelvic floor muscles and improving bladder control, Emsella helps you sleep through the night without interruption. This improvement in sleep quality is one of the most life-changing benefits our patients experience.
